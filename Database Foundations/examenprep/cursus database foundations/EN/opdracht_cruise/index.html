Group work modelling

Assignment

Within this course, you learned what a database is, how to model it, implement it, and how to write queries to add and extract data in and out of the database. In this project you will go through the entire process from data model to queries for a specific case. The case is described below, it is up to you to develop the requested database according to the learned process.

This project will be realized by groups of maximum 4 students. The groups will be determined by the lecturers. The deadline for submission of your result is on December 13, 2024 at 11:59 p.m.. You need to submit the result via the assignment ‘Project Assignment’ which you can find on Toledo. This project counts for 5 points. A project that is not submitted by the deadline will result in a 0. Retakes are not possible.

For the second exam chance, the points on the group assignment do not carry over and the exam consequently counts for 18 points. After all, there are two points on the portfolio and those are retained. For more info: see ECTS sheet (link on Toledo or via your ISP) and Toledo.

To be handed in

You need to work out the following things:

  1. A conceptual data model in which you develop the case below. The conceptual data model needs to be drawn in draw.io and submitted in pdf format with the name ‘DF_conceptual_team_XX.pdf’ where you replace XX with your team number.
  2. A logical data model that contains the translation from your conceptual data model to a relational database model. The logical data model needs to be drawn in draw.io and submitted in pdf format with the name ‘DF_logical_team_XX.pdf’.
  3. A physical data model that contains the translation from your logical data model to a database in PostgreSQL. You need to create the physical data model preferably in DBSchema and submit the resulting script with the name ‘DF_create_database_team_XX.sql’. Also provide a .png of your schema (in DBSchema: Model > Export as ... > PNG image) with the name ‘DF_physical_team_XX.png’.
  4. A second script that adds data to the created tables. This script, named ‘DF_insert_data_team_XX.sql’, populates the database.
  5. A third script ‘DF_delete_database_team_XX.sql’, that clears the created schema. It deletes the schema. You may not use a drop cascade, but must delete all the tables separately in the correct order.
  6. Five queries that extract a result from the database (see below). You collect the different queries in one script ‘DF_query_team_XX.sql’.
  7. A video of maximum 15 minutes named ‘DF_video_team_XX’ In which you give a demo of your final results (described above). The format of the video must be playable in VLC. In this video, first of all, you explain your models and your reasoning behind them. After showing the physical data model you build the whole database through pgAdmin by using your scripts (table creation and data). Then you show the execution of your queries and finally you delete the schema with your delete script. Test your video recording setup well in advance (software, video format, microphone, ...)!

Bundle all of the files above into one ‘DF_project_team_XX.zip’ file. Submit this .zip file via Toledo (Assignments > Project (group)). One submission per group is sufficient.

Cruise holidays

The case below describes a company, people, objects and processes. We intentionally left out a lot of details. What information does one want to track of a ship, passenger, activity, ... ? Make meaningful choices yourself and motivate them.

A shipping company operates a number of cruise ships and organises cruise holidays with them. For this assignment, it is a good idea to check out some cruise holiday providers and read up a bit on this world.

A number of details are kept of each ship, such as the name, the number of passengers, etc. The aim for a ship is to do as many cruise holidays as possible and lie idle as little as possible (although some occasional maintenance is necesarry of course).

One specific cruise holiday is carried out with one ship. One, of course, always tries to come up with a catchy name for the holiday. The holiday follows a fixed sailing route with a departure and arrival date. The trip can be performed several times. For example, the voyage ‘Around Iceland’ will be done as many as seven times in 2025, at different times and not necessarily with the same ship.

Such a shipping route visits several ports, in a specific order. Your database should be able to answer, for example, the following question: “Give for cruise voyage ‘MSC-Scandinavia-026a’ start and arrival date, the name of the ship and a list of all ports in the correct order.” This trip appears to depart on July 7, 2025 from Antwerp with the ship ‘Icon of the Seas’. The ship will then enter these ports: Amsterdam, Hamburg, Copenhagen, Oslo, Geirangerfjord, Trondheim, South Queensferry and Newcastle to end in Amsterdam on 17 July 2025.

Not every port is big enough (deep actually) to accommodate such a large ship. Sometimes, if the ship's depth is greater than the depth of the port, the ship has to remain moored outside of the port and passengers for an excursion are brought ashore by a smaller boat. So it is important to know the depth of each ship.

The aim is to get as many passengers as possible on a cruise, without exceeding the maximum capacity of the cruise ship. The company must keep sufficient details of every passenger (make your own motivated choice of what all might be required). It is possible for several people to book together (think of a family, a group of friends, an association, ...), but there is always only one booking per group. The person who made that booking is the first point of contact for the shipping company for questions, invoices, etc. For example, I should be able to find in the database that Per Olov Enquist from Stockholm is a loyal customer of our company. He has already done a total of 5 cruises, the last one being the voyage ‘Culture in the Mediterranean’ from early August 2023 (departure in Barcelona). He did that cruise in the company of three friends, for which passenger Viveca Sten from Gothenburg was the main booker.

On such a cruise, most things are included in the price, but some things can be booked extra. For example, on that last trip Per Olov Enquist consulted the doctor on board (€50) and did a paid day trip in Venice (€185) on 7 August 2023. Those costs will therefore be passed on to the main booker Viveca Sten, who will find that in detail on the final invoice.

At some ports, many people board buses that take them sightseeing. In Reykjavik, for example, many passengers visit Geysir to watch Strokkur, or bathe in the warm volcanic waters of The Blue lagoon. The town of Akureyri in Northern Iceland is a good base for spotting whales from small boats. For every port, there should be a list of interesting sights that are at most a few hours' bus ride away from the port.

Perhaps a passenger prefers to stay on the ship, where there is no shortage of comfort. Each ship has a lot of different room types. The more expensive rooms obviously have a balcony with sea views, the cheaper ones have to make do with a bit less. There are single rooms, but also rooms for 2, 3, ... passengers. Who knows, the ship may also have special family rooms where the children can share the room with their parents? For each room type, there are many rooms, all of which have a unique code. Thus I can find that on his last cruise trip in the Mediterranean Sea, Per Olov Enquist travelled in room A306. That is a more expensive room of the ‘Balcony’ type for two people.

Data Generation

You need to fill all tables with sufficient data. That data should look realistic. Usually, a combination of manual work (typing in data yourself) and automatic data generation (via DBSchema's data generator or online tools like Mockaroo) delivers the best results. What is "sufficient", by the way? Hard to put a specific number on that ("You must have at least ... rows in that table"). We would say: When querying (see next point), you should be able to get meaningful results. For example, the tennis player database is really not large enough (only 14 players, 2 teams, 8 fines). The cooking club, on the other hand, is sufficiently large (173 partially hand-coded ingredients, 500 automatically generated members, 10 manually entered dishes, 1178 communities found via a CSV file on the site of bpost, ...)

Queries

Compile 5 sufficiently complex and meaningful queries to query your database. Try to use all SQL material: SELECT, WHERE, inner and outer JOINS, GROUP BY, HAVING, ORDER BY, limit number of rows, etc.

We emphasized two words here:

  1. complex: we really do expect queries that employ the whole arsenal of what we saw in the SQL classes and not just a statement where you select something from a table.
  2. meaningful: justify why for the shipping company the result of this query is important. Introduce each query with some explanation, e.g. ‘For the shpping company it is important to get an overview per ... because ...’.
